General Rules and Guidelines

ARTICLE I: Purpose, Description and Implementation

SECTION 1 Event Branding - The event shall always be known as “CS Days” proceeded by the current event theme.
SECTION 2: Event Logo - The CS Days logo shall always be the logo of the event. It shall always be visible on any publication materials and event materials that includes certificates, slides, trophy et.al, in whole.
SECTION 3: Definition of Terms - As used in this General Rules and Regulations:
--- A. “Event” shall mean the event itself.
--- B. “Competition(s)” shall mean the competitions from the four (4) pillars.
--- C. “Participant(s)” shall mean all enrolled CS students, alumni and CS faculty.
--- D. “Player(s)” shall mean all eligible participants from any competition.
--- E. “Schedule Competition Time” shall mean the specified time of the start of the competition as stipulated on the Competition Rules and Regulations of each competition.
--- F. “Official Competition Time” shall mean the actual time that the competition will start. The time is not fixed, it will depend on the current situation of the competition.
--- G. “Main Player” shall mean the player who will officially play the competition itself.
--- H. “Substitute Player” shall mean the player who will substitute the main player in times when the main player is injured, or late.
--- I. “Participation Point” shall mean the point given to players who are unable to reach 2nd runner up, 1st runner up and champion.
--- J. “Ranking Point” shall mean the point given to players who are declared as the 2nd runner up, 1st runner up and champion.
SECTION 4: Description - The CS Days is an annual event competition of the College of Computer Studies (CCS) for the purpose of developing problem solving and coding skills, displaying leadership skills, with an emphasis on developing social skills as well as to have fun and win.
SECTION 5: Supporting Pillars - The event shall always be supported by the following pillars:
--- A. Think - enable students to prove and test their problem solving skills as well as display considerable leadership skills;
--- B. Play - encourage students to blow off their steam and have fun on a variety of sports and video games;
--- C. Code - to challenge and show off their current skills on programming;
--- D. Meet - to emphasize and practice the importance of communication and establishing relationships and connections, skills that are essential for the industry;
--- E. Win - of course it would not be a competition without prizes.
SECTION 6: Event Theme - The event shall have the theme “CS Days: A Digital Day 0ff” for the academic year 2022-2023.
SECTION 7: The event theme shall be amended or revised every year in accordance or strongly related to the current academic year. Reusing old themes is allowed given that it is at least three (3) years old.
SECTION 8: Implementing Body - The Computer Studies College Council (CSCC) shall be the implementing body of the event.
SECTION 9: Organizations affiliated with the CCS may be granted, upon approval of the Computer Studies (CS) faculty and CSCC, the privilege to co-host the event.
SECTION 10: Event Duration - The duration of the event shall be at least two (2) and at most three (3) academic days as scheduled by the CSCC and approved by the CS faculty.

ARTICLE II: Participants and Teams

SECTION 1: Participants - All enrolled CS students from any year level and course, alumni and CS faculty shall be part of the event.
SECTION 2: All enrolled CS students shall at least participate in at least one (1) competition.
SECTION 3: Team Creation - Participants shall be randomly teamed using a random list generator.
SECTION 4: The team shall be composed of:
--- A. Six (6) teams.
--- B. Each team shall consist of at least 38 CS Students excluding CSCC officers.
--- C. The teams shall be composed of:
--- --- a. 17-18 Freshmen;
--- --- b. 9-10 Sophomore;
--- --- c. 4-5 Junior and;
--- --- d. 7-8 Senior.
--- --- D. The year level shall be based on the year level printout as provided by the CS Faculty.
--- E. CSCC officers shall be distributed to each team randomly.
--- F. Each team shall have at least one (1) and at most two (2) CS faculty.
--- G. Each team shall have at least one (1) or more alumni.
SECTION 5: Each team shall have a team name and team logo based on the computer studies values and personality traits.
SECTION 6: The requirement on ARTICLE II SECTION 5 shall be submitted on or before April 15, 2023 Friday 12:00 MN.

ARTICLE IV: Eligibility of Players

SECTION 1: Eligibility of Players - A player is allowed to participate in at most six (6) competitions.
SECTION 2: A player shall follow requirements as stipulated on the Competition Rules and Guidelines of a particular competition.

ARTICLE V: General Provisions

SECTION 1: The competitions are regulated by the following rules and guidelines, which apply to all players. We reserve the right to change and amend the following rules and guidelines before the competition starting date and time. It is the players’ responsibility to read the rules and be up to date.
SECTION 2: It is the player’s responsibility to check the date and time of the competition they are registered in.
SECTION 3: Point System - The event shall implement the following point system:
--- A. Points will be divided into two categories: Participation Point and Ranking Point.
--- B. The definition of Participation Point and Ranking Point is stated in ARTICLE I SECTION 3.
--- C. All competition shall give points to players.
--- D. The number of points varies on each competition.
--- E. Each main player will automatically receive one (1) participation point.
--- F. The champion will receive the 100% ranking point given to the competition
--- G. The 1st runner up will receive the 75% of the ranking points given to the competition.
--- H. The 2nd runner up will receive the 50% of the ranking points given to the competition.
--- I. Competitions that require to create teams shall only receive one (1) participation point no matter the number of players in a team.
SECTION 4: Postponement - All competitions shall be played as scheduled by the CSCC. No request for postponement of games except for reasons beyond control of man shall be allowed after the schedule has been released.
SECTION 5: Line Up Changes - No change in the line-up shall be made after a team or player has started playing. A team fielding a player whose name does not appear in the entry form submitted or using the name of a registered one shall be disqualified from further participation in the competition.
SECTION 6: Basic Courtesy - A player should be at the venue five (5) minutes before the scheduled competition time.
SECTION 7: If a player has any issues five (5) minutes before the start of the scheduled competition time, it is their responsibility to contact the competition manager.
SECTION 8: Default - A player that fails to appear three (3) minutes after the start of the official competition time shall be declared in default.
SECTION 9: Sanctions to players shall be applied by the competition manager immediately or after the consideration of all case circumstances.
SECTION 10: The following cases are considered violation of rules, and any player found liable of such violation can be disqualified at the discretion of the competition managers.
--- A. Intentionally abusing a loophole on the guidelines.
--- B. Rude or disrespectful behavior from players, including but not limited to flaming, raging, trolling, rage-quitting, bad-mouthing, during the competition or in conversation before or after the competition.
--- C. Ignoring the requests of the competition managers.
--- D. Rude or disrespectful behavior towards a competition manager.
SECTION 11: Sanctions may include but aren’t limited to: disqualification or player ban from other competitions.
SECTION 12: All protests regarding violation of the General Rules and Guidelines and Competition Rules and Guidelines shall be submitted to the CS Office thirty (30) minutes after the competition has concluded.
SECTION 13: All cases not explicitly mentioned in the General Rules and Guidelines and Competition Rules and Guidelines will be dealt with individually by the organizers and competition managers. All organizers' and competition managers’ decisions must be respected and executed without delay.
